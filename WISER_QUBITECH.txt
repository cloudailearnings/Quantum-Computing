Day 3 | Quantum Computing – Quantum Fundamentals & Advanced Algorithms Program
Day 3 focused on understanding how quantum computing connects with classical machine learning and real-world energy systems.
Highlights from Day 3:
1.Classical ML vs Quantum ML
Learned how classical machine learning can often match quantum ML for many problems, while quantum computing (BQP) opens possibilities beyond classical probabilistic algorithms (BPP).
2.Smart Grid Evolution
 Explored key properties of modern smart grids, including:
 • Diverse energy sources
 • Increased electrification and consumption points
 • Decentralized energy generation and resource allocation
3.Quantum Computing for Energy Applications
Introduced to Quantum Machine Learning for energy demand forecasting, showing how quantum approaches can support efficient planning and grid optimization.
Key Takeaway:
Quantum computing is not about replacing classical methods—but augmenting them to solve complex, data-intensive energy challenges more effectively.
Grateful to continue learning and exploring how quantum technologies can shape the future of energy systems.
