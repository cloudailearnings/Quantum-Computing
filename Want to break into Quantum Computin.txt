Want to break into Quantum Computing but don’t know where to start? You’re not alone and the good news is getting started is easier than you think.

After 3+ years in quantum, building projects, doing research, hackathons, internships, and creating content for thousands of learners, here’s the roadmap I wish I had on Day 1. 

⭐ Step 1 - Build Strong Math Foundations
Linear Algebra, Complex Numbers, Probability
This is 70% of the game. Don’t skip it.

⭐ Step 2 - Learn Core Quantum Concepts
• Superposition 
• Entanglement 
• Measurement
Just enough physics to start coding real quantum circuits.

⭐ Step 3 - Learn Python + a Quantum SDK
• Qiskit 
• PennyLane 
• Cirq
Start with simple circuits → experiment → visualize.

⭐ Step 4 - Implement the Fundamental Algorithms
• Grover’s 
• Shor’s 
• QFT 
• Deutsch-Jozsa etc.
These give you the quantum intuition no textbook alone can teach.

⭐ Step 5 - Explore Advanced Areas
Quantum Machine Learning (QML)
 Quantum Cryptography (QKD, PQC)
 Quantum Error Correction etc.
Pick one track and go deep.

⭐ Step 6 - Build Real Projects + Join Communities
• Hackathons 
• Research Internships 
• Quantum Discords/Slack 
• Open-source
This is where the real growth and opportunities happen.
